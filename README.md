# Japanese Nihonto Forging Registry

This registry documents traditional Japanese nihonto forging techniques on-chain.  
It focuses on historically documented schools and practices rather than modern or commercial interpretations of the katana.

## Purpose

The goal of this contract is to preserve core elements of nihonto forging in a permanent and verifiable way.  
Forging knowledge is often transmitted through apprenticeship and family lineages, and much of it is vulnerable to loss, simplification, or distortion.

By recording selected techniques on-chain, this registry creates a stable reference that links:

- a region or forging area  
- a school or lineage  
- a named technique  
- the material used  
- a cultural or historical note  
- its traditional or symbolic purpose  

## Why this contract matters

The katana is widely recognized, but its cultural and technical background is frequently reduced to clichés.  
This registry is not about glorifying weapons. It is about preserving the craft logic behind nihonto forging as a cultural and historical practice.

The contract helps to:

- keep a neutral and transparent record of authentic techniques  
- attach cultural context to each recorded practice  
- provide a reference for researchers, collectors, and students of traditional forging  
- resist the loss of nuance that comes with purely commercial or fictional representations  

## What makes this registry unique

This registry is narrowly focused on forging techniques, not on finished swords as objects of status or consumption.  
It treats the process itself as the cultural asset: smelting, folding, heat treatment, finishing, and their meanings inside specific schools.

The design is minimal on purpose.  
It does not try to catalogue every detail, only the essential information needed to identify and contextualize a technique.

## How it works

Each entry includes:

- region  
- school  
- technique name  
- material type  
- cultural note  
- ritual or traditional use  

A simple like and dislike mechanism allows basic feedback and visibility for entries.
